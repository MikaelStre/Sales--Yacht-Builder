101 Hull structures

The hull shell will be supported by transverse and longitudinal structures.

The high-stress areas, such as the ones surrounding the pivoting daggerboards, lower rudder bearings, bow thruster, crash bow, chain plates and other stressed areas are reinforced with additional laminate and dedicated structures that conform with high latitude passages.

Hull internal structures and bulkheads will be provided with limber holes flush to hull, allowing all water to drain to the dedicated sumps. Limber will be arranged where needed in order to provide drainage. The holes will be finished so that the foam core and bulkhead edges are not exposed.

Structural engineering to be done by Rivoyre Ingenierie.

Hull will be NDT checked and report to be shared with owner’s representative and structural engineering company and if any repairs needed it has to be agreed between all three parties and any eventually repair considered covered by the yard.

Structural bulkheads

All bulkheads have a sandwich construction with skins made of woven and biaxial standard modulus carbon epoxy sprint fabrics with corecell core. Unidirectional fibres used in local reinforcements where needed. All bulkheads will be according to classification societies rules.

The bulkheads are manufactured on a vacuum table, to be flat and distortion free. All the laminate layers are accurately cut using our automated and computerized cutting machine. 20 Deck

Section owner: Mechanical / Deck

General concept

The deck will have a superstructure including a flybridge.

The deck will incorporate a toe rail in composite, details still to be defined. The toe rail will be reinforced at the location of cleats and other strong points.

The flybridge will have twin helm stations one (1) port side, one (1) stbd side. Each helm station will have its own steering pedestal with equipment as described in section 80. The pedestals will be mirrored images of each other.

The area forward incorporates a comfortable relaxing cockpit. There is also an aft cockpit area for a more sheltered relaxing area. The flybridge offers a good view for helmsman and with a sunbed arrangement.

The main tender is stored on a tenderlift most aft. The crew tender is stored un-asembled and deflated in the port side forepeak.

401 Engine room

General

The engine rooms are internally insulated for both fire safety and sound reduction. All equipment and insulation will be installed according to the insulation plan and the advice of noise and vibration consultants.

Access to the engine rooms will be through watertight deck hatches. These hatches will have Baltic custom profiles, ensuring they offer the same level of watertightness, fire resistance, and noise reduction as the engine room panels.

The walls and ceilings of the engine room will be clad with BONDAL Aluminum plating. Accessibility will be ensured to allow for the servicing of all equipment.

The engine room insulation will consist of two layers of high-density mineral wool (approved to A60 standard), with damping aluminum sheets and perforated sheets in designated areas.

The engine room floor will be made of GRP or Aluminum plates painted with non-skid paint and landed on nitrile gaskets glued to the bearers. Individual floor plates will be sized for easy removal and temporary storage to access under-sole equipment and systems.

If space permits A workbench with a vice will be installed in each engine room. With a fold-up washbasin plumbed with hot and cold water will be installed at the workbench.

Pipes in the engine room, machinery spaces, and other areas containing valves and shut-offs will be painted according to the relevant space specifications. Plastic pipe fittings that disassemble will not be painted. Valves, cocks, filling caps, etc., will have their handles, levers, or covers paint…
